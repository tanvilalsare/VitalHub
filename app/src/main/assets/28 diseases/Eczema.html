Eczema (Atopic Dermatitis)

Atopic dermatitis (eczema) is a condition that causes dry, itchy, and inflamed skin. It is common in young children but can occur at any age. It is long-lasting (chronic) and tends to flare occasionally. While eczema can be irritating, it is not contagious.

Symptoms
  • Dry, cracked skin
  • Itchiness (pruritus)
  • Rash on swollen skin that varies in color depending on your skin tone
  • Small, raised bumps, especially on brown or black skin
  • Oozing and crusting
  • Thickened skin
  • Darkening of the skin around the eyes
  • Raw, sensitive skin from scratching
Complications
  • Asthma and hay fever
  • Food allergies
  • Chronic itchy, scaly skin
  • Patches of skin that are darker or lighter than the surrounding area
  • Skin infections
  • Irritant hand dermatitis
  • Allergic contact dermatitis
  • Sleep problems
  • Mental health conditions
Prevention
  • Moisturize your skin at least twice a day
  • Take a daily bath or shower
  • Use a gentle, non-soap cleanser
  • Pat dry your skin instead of rubbing it
Triggers
  • Rough wool fabric
  • Dry skin
  • Skin infections
  • Heat and sweat
  • Stress
  • Cleaning products
  • Dust mites and pet dander
  • Mold
  • Pollen
  • Smoke from tobacco
  • Cold and dry air
  • Fragrances
  • Other irritating chemicals
Treatment
Medication
  • Skin-care products like creams, gels, and ointments to control itching and repair the skin.
  • Calcineurin inhibitors: Tacrolimus (Protopic) and pimecrolimus (Elidel) recommended for children over 2 years old.
  • Antibiotic pills: May be prescribed to treat infections.
  • Pills controlling inflammation: Options include cyclosporine, methotrexate, prednisone, mycophenolate, and azathioprine.
  • Injectable biologics: Dupilumab (Dupixent) and tralokinumab (Adbry) for moderate to severe disease. Studies show they are effective in easing eczema symptoms.
Information about Medication
  • Common Protopic side effects may include: burning, stinging, itching, or tingling of treated skin (especially during the first few days of treatment), viral skin infection, increased skin sensitivity to hot or cold temperatures, acne, upset stomach, headache, muscle pain, and other symptoms like fever or sore throat.
  • Common side effects of pimecrolimus: burning, stinging, or soreness of treated skin, viral skin infection, or respiratory issues.
  • Adbry Side Effects: Upper respiratory tract infections, eye and eyelid inflammation, injection site reactions, and high white blood cell counts.
  • Dupixent Side Effects: Pain or swelling at the injection site, upper respiratory infections, eye problems (inflammation, redness, blurred vision), and other side effects such as headache and muscle pain.

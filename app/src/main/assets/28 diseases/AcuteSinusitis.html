Acute Sinusitis

What is Acute Sinusitis?

Acute sinusitis causes the spaces inside the nose, known as sinuses, to become inflamed and swollen, making it difficult for the sinuses to drain. Mucus builds up, leading to a variety of symptoms. It is typically caused by infections, allergens, or irritants.

Symptoms

  • Thick, yellow or greenish mucus from the nose or down the back of the throat (postnasal drip).
  • Blocked or stuffy nose that makes it difficult to breathe.
  • Pain, tenderness, swelling, and pressure around the eyes, cheeks, nose, or forehead, which worsens when bending over.
  • Ear pressure, headache, aching in the teeth, changed sense of smell, cough, bad breath, tiredness, and fever.

Prevention

  • Stay well and avoid people with colds or infections.
  • Wash hands frequently, especially before meals.
  • Manage allergies with your healthcare provider.
  • Avoid cigarette smoke and polluted air to prevent irritation of nasal passages.
  • Use a humidifier to add moisture to dry home air, keeping the nasal passages moist and clean.

Treatment

  • Saline nasal spray: Rinses the nose with saltwater to keep it clear.
  • Nasal corticosteroids: Prevent and treat swelling (e.g., fluticasone, budesonide).
  • Decongestants: Available in liquids, tablets, and nasal sprays; use for a few days to avoid rebound congestion.
  • Allergy medicines: To manage symptoms in sinusitis caused by allergies.
  • Pain relievers: Acetaminophen, ibuprofen, or aspirin to alleviate pain.

Appendicitis

Symptoms

Common Symptoms:
  • Sudden pain starting on the lower right side of the abdomen.
  • Pain that begins around the belly button and shifts to the lower right abdomen.
  • Pain that worsens with coughing, walking, or making sudden movements.
  • Nausea and vomiting.
  • Loss of appetite.
  • Low-grade fever that may worsen.
  • Constipation or diarrhea.
  • Bloating or gas.

Risk Factors

  • Age: Most common between 10 and 30 years old.
  • Sex: Men are at slightly higher risk than women.

Complications

  • Ruptured appendix: Can lead to peritonitis, requiring immediate surgery.
  • Abscess: Infection pocket that forms after a rupture, often needing drainage.
  • Infection removal: The appendix is removed surgically once infection is cleared.

Treatment

  • Surgery to remove the appendix.
  • Draining of an abscess before appendix surgery if needed.

Lifestyle and Home Remedies

  • Limit activity for laparoscopic appendectomy patients for 3 to 5 days.
  • Limit activity for open appendectomy patients for 10 to 14 days.
  • Support your abdomen when coughing to reduce pain.
  • Contact your healthcare team if pain medicines aren't helping.
  • Gradually increase activity after starting to move again.
  • Sleep when tired to help your body heal.
  • Consult your healthcare team about when to return to work or school.
  • Children can return to school within a week, but certain activities may be delayed for 2 to 4 weeks.

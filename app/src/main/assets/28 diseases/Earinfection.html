Ear Infection

Symptoms
Children
  • Ear pain, especially when lying down
  • Tugging or pulling at an ear
  • Trouble sleeping
  • Crying more than usual
  • Fussiness
  • Trouble hearing or responding to sounds
  • Loss of balance
  • Fever of 100°F (38°C) or higher
  • Drainage of fluid from the ear
  • Headache
  • Loss of appetite
Adults
  • Ear pain
  • Drainage of fluid from the ear
  • Trouble hearing
Risk Factors
  • Age
  • Group child care
  • Infant feeding
  • Seasonal factors
  • Poor air quality
  • Alaska native heritage
  • Cleft palate
Complications
  • Impaired hearing
  • Speech or developmental delays
  • Tearing of the eardrum
Prevention
  • Prevent common colds and other illnesses
  • Avoid secondhand smoke
  • Breastfeed your baby
Treatment
Children
  • Some ear infections resolve without antibiotic treatment, depending on the child's age and severity of symptoms.
  • A wait-and-see approach is recommended for children 6 to 23 months with mild middle ear pain and a temperature below 102.2°F (39°C).
  • Antibiotics may be helpful for some children with ear infections, but excessive use can lead to antibiotic resistance.
  • Pain management treatments include over-the-counter pain medication like acetaminophen or ibuprofen, and anesthetic drops if the eardrum doesn't have a hole or tear.
  • Antibiotic therapy may be recommended for children 6 months and older with moderate to severe ear pain or a temperature below 102.2°F (39°C) or higher.

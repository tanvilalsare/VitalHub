Urinary Tract Infection (UTI)

A urinary tract infection (UTI) is an infection that affects any part of the urinary system, including the kidneys, ureters, bladder, and urethra. The most common UTIs involve the lower urinary tract â€” the bladder and urethra.

Symptoms
  • A strong, persistent urge to urinate
  • A burning sensation during urination
  • Frequent urination with small amounts of urine
  • Cloudy or foul-smelling urine
  • Urine that appears red, pink, or cola-colored (indicating blood)
  • Pelvic pain (in women), especially around the pubic bone area
Part of Urinary Tract Symptoms
Kidneys Back or side pain, high fever, chills, nausea, vomiting
Bladder Pelvic pressure, lower belly discomfort, frequent and painful urination, blood in urine
Urethra Burning sensation during urination, discharge
Risk Factors
  • Female anatomy: Shorter urethra in women allows bacteria to reach the bladder more easily.
  • Sexual activity: Increased activity or new partners can increase risk.
  • Certain birth control methods: Diaphragms and spermicides can elevate UTI risk.
  • Menopause: Hormonal changes after menopause can increase susceptibility.
  • Urinary tract problems: Conditions like kidney stones or congenital abnormalities may increase risk.
  • Suppressed immune system: Conditions like diabetes can impair immunity, raising the risk.
  • Catheter use: People using catheters have an increased UTI risk.
  • Recent urinary procedures: Surgeries or exams related to the urinary system can elevate risk.
Prevention
  • Drink plenty of water to stay hydrated
  • Wipe from front to back after using the toilet
  • Urinate soon after sexual intercourse
  • Avoid irritating feminine products (e.g., douches or sprays)
  • Consider changing your birth control method if necessary
Treatment
Medications:
  • Trimethoprim and Sulfamethoxazole
  • Fosfomycin
  • Nitrofurantoin
  • Cephalexin
  • Ceftriaxone
Possible Side Effects:
  • Trimethoprim/Sulfamethoxazole: Nausea, vomiting, loss of appetite, skin rash
  • Fosfomycin: Nausea, upset stomach, diarrhea, headache, dizziness
  • Nitrofurantoin: Headache, dizziness, stomach upset, mild diarrhea
  • Cephalexin: Diarrhea, nausea, indigestion, stomach pain, vaginal itching
  • Ceftriaxone: Diarrhea, rash, symptoms of a blood disorder, liver issues

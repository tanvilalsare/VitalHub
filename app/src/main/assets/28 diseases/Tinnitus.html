Tinnitus

What is Tinnitus?

Tinnitus is often caused by an underlying condition, such as age-related hearing loss, ear injury, or circulatory problems. For many, tinnitus improves with treatment of the underlying cause or treatments that reduce or mask the noise, making it less noticeable.

Symptoms

  • Buzzing
  • Roaring
  • Clicking
  • Hissing
  • Humming

Common Causes of Tinnitus

  • Hearing loss: Caused by damaged hair cells in the inner ear.
  • Ear infection or ear canal blockage: Buildup of earwax or foreign materials causing pressure change in the ear.
  • Head or neck injuries: Trauma affecting the inner ear, hearing nerves, or brain.
  • Medications: Certain medications like NSAIDs, antibiotics, cancer drugs, water pills, antimalarial drugs, and antidepressants.

Other Causes of Tinnitus

  • Meniere's disease: Abnormal inner ear fluid pressure.
  • Eustachian tube dysfunction: The ear tube stays expanded, causing the ear to feel full.
  • Ear bone changes: Stiffening of bones in the middle ear affecting hearing.
  • Muscle spasms in the inner ear: Inner ear muscles tense up causing tinnitus.
  • Temporomandibular joint (TMJ) disorders: Problems with the TMJ can cause tinnitus.
  • Acoustic neuroma or head/neck tumors: Tumors affecting the ear and brain.
  • Blood vessel disorders: Conditions affecting blood vessels causing forceful blood flow.

Risk Factors

  • Loud noise exposure: Prolonged exposure to loud sounds can cause hearing loss.
  • Age: Nerve fiber degeneration in the ears due to aging.
  • Sex: Men are more likely to experience tinnitus.
  • Tobacco and alcohol use: Increases the risk of tinnitus.
  • Health problems: Obesity, cardiovascular issues, high blood pressure, and previous head injuries increase the risk.

Prevention

  • Use hearing protection: Avoid loud environments or use ear protection.
  • Turn down the volume: Avoid prolonged exposure to high-volume music or sounds.
  • Maintain cardiovascular health: Healthy exercise, diet, and blood vessel health can help prevent tinnitus.
  • Limit alcohol, caffeine, and nicotine: These can worsen tinnitus symptoms.

Treatment

  • Earwax removal: Can alleviate tinnitus symptoms caused by blocked ear canals.
  • Treatment of blood vessel conditions: May require medications or surgery.
  • Hearing aids: Can improve tinnitus symptoms caused by hearing loss.
  • Medication changes: Switching medications if they are causing tinnitus symptoms.

Noise Suppression

  • White noise machines: Produce soothing background noise to mask tinnitus.
  • Masking devices: Wearable devices that produce low-level white noise to reduce tinnitus perception.

Counseling

  • Tinnitus Retraining Therapy (TRT): Combines sound masking with counseling.
  • Cognitive Behavioral Therapy (CBT): Helps cope with tinnitus and associated conditions like anxiety and depression.

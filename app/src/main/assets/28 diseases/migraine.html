Migraine

Symptoms

Migraines, which affect children and teenagers as well as adults, can progress through four stages: prodrome, aura, attack and post-drome. Not everyone who has migraines goes through all stages.

Prodrome
  • Constipation
  • Mood changes, from depression to euphoria
  • Food cravings
  • Neck stiffness
  • Increased urination
  • Fluid retention
  • Frequent yawning
Aura
  • Visual phenomena, such as seeing various shapes, bright spots or flashes of light
  • Vision loss
  • Pins and needles sensations in an arm or leg
  • Weakness or numbness in the face or one side of the body
  • Difficulty speaking
Attack
  • Pain usually on one side of your head, but often on both sides
  • Pain that throbs or pulses
  • Sensitivity to light, sound, and sometimes smell and touch
  • Nausea and vomiting
Post-drome
  • After a migraine attack, you might feel drained, confused, and washed out for up to a day. Some people report feeling elated.
  • Sudden head movement might bring on the pain again briefly.

Causes

  • Hormonal changes in women: Fluctuations in estrogen during menstruation, pregnancy, and menopause can trigger migraines.
  • Drinks: Alcohol, especially wine, and excessive caffeine can worsen migraines.
  • Stress: Work or home stress can cause migraines.
  • Sensory stimuli: Bright lights, loud sounds, strong smells can trigger migraines.
  • Sleep changes: Missing or excessive sleep can trigger migraines.
  • Physical strain: Intense physical exertion, including sexual activity, can provoke migraines.
  • Weather changes: Changes in weather or barometric pressure can trigger a migraine.
  • Medicines: Oral contraceptives and vasodilators can aggravate migraines.
  • Foods: Aged cheeses, salty and processed foods, and skipping meals can trigger migraines.

Treatment

Medication
  • Pain Relievers – Aspirin, ibuprofen
  • Triptans – sumatriptan, rizatriptan
  • Dihydroergotamine
  • Lasmitidan
  • Gepants
  • Zavzpret
Side Effects of Medication
  • Aspirin: Upset stomach, heartburn, drowsiness, or mild headache.
  • Ibuprofen: Nausea, vomiting, gas, bleeding, or dizziness.
  • Sumatriptan: Numbness, tingling, warmth, redness, dizziness, neck pain or stiffness.
  • Rizatriptan: Dizziness, drowsiness, pain in the throat or chest.
  • Dihydroergotamine: Pain, bruising, swelling, nausea, diarrhea, runny nose, altered taste.
  • Lasmitidan: Dizziness, sleepiness, numbness, tiredness, tingling.

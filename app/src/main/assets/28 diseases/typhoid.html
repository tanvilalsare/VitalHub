Typhoid Fever

Cause

Typhoid fever is caused by bacteria called Salmonella.

Symptoms

Symptoms usually appear 1 to 3 weeks after exposure to the bacteria, with an initial slow onset.

Early Illness Symptoms
  • Fever that gradually rises, potentially reaching up to 104°F (40°C).
  • Chills
  • Headache
  • Weakness and fatigue
  • Muscle aches
  • Stomach pain
  • Diarrhea or constipation
  • Rash
  • Cough, loss of appetite, and sweating may also occur.
Later Illness Symptoms
  • Severe stomach pain and swelling.
  • Sepsis (infection spreading throughout the body).
  • In very serious cases, confusion and inability to respond to surroundings.

Complications

  • Intestinal damage and bleeding.
  • Sepsis (infection spreading throughout the body).
  • Myocarditis (inflammation of the heart muscle), endocarditis (inflammation of the heart valves), and mycotic aneurysm.
  • Pneumonia, pancreatitis, and kidney or bladder infections.
  • Meningitis, psychiatric problems like delirium, hallucinations, and psychosis.

Prevention

  • Get vaccinated against typhoid fever.
  • Wash your hands frequently with soap.
  • Avoid untreated water sources.
  • Avoid raw fruits and vegetables unless they can be peeled or washed properly.
  • Choose hot foods over cold foods to minimize risk.

Medications

  • Fluoroquinolones - ciprofloxacin
  • Cephalosporins
  • Macrolides - azithromycin
  • Carbapenems

Precautions and Side Effects for Medications

Fluoroquinolone
  • May cause tendon problems, nerve damage, and changes in mood or behavior.
  • Low blood sugar may occur.
  • Do not take if allergic to ciprofloxacin or if you also take tizanidine.
Cephalosporins
  • May cause abdominal pain, diarrhea, dyspepsia, headache, gastritis, nausea, and vomiting.
Macrolides
  • May cause diarrhea, nausea, vomiting, stomach pain, and headaches.

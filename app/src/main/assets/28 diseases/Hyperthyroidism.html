Hyperthyroidism

Overview

Hyperthyroidism happens when the thyroid gland makes too much thyroid hormone. This condition is also called overactive thyroid. Hyperthyroidism speeds up the body's metabolism.

Symptoms

Hyperthyroidism sometimes looks like other health problems, making it hard to diagnose. It can cause many symptoms, including:

  • Losing weight without trying
  • Fast heartbeat (tachycardia)
  • Irregular heartbeat (arrhythmia)
  • Pounding of the heart (heart palpitations)
  • Increased hunger
  • Nervousness, anxiety, and irritability
  • Tremor (small trembling in the hands and fingers)
  • Sweating
  • Changes in menstrual cycles
  • Increased sensitivity to heat
  • Changes in bowel patterns, especially more frequent bowel movements
  • Enlarged thyroid gland (goiter), which may appear as swelling at the base of the neck
  • Tiredness
  • Muscle weakness
  • Sleep problems
  • Warm, moist skin
  • Thinning skin
  • Fine, brittle hair

Risk Factors

Risk factors for hyperthyroidism include:

  • A family history of thyroid disease, particularly Graves' disease
  • A personal history of certain chronic illnesses, including pernicious anemia and primary adrenal insufficiency
  • A recent pregnancy, which raises the risk of developing thyroiditis that can lead to hyperthyroidism

Treatment

  • Anti-thyroid medicine (e.g., methimazole and propylthiouracil)
  • Beta blockers
  • Radioiodine therapy
  • Thyroidectomy (surgical removal of the thyroid gland)

Side Effects of Anti-Thyroid Medicines

Methimazole Side Effects

Seek emergency help if you experience:

  • Allergic reactions (e.g., hives, difficulty breathing, swelling of the face, lips, tongue, or throat)
  • Signs of infection (e.g., sudden weakness, fever, chills, sore throat)
  • Severe side effects like swollen glands, liver problems, or unusual bleeding

Common side effects:

  • Nausea, vomiting, upset stomach
  • Headache, dizziness, drowsiness
  • Rash, itching, skin discoloration
  • Muscle or joint pain
  • Hair loss
  • Decreased sense of taste

Propylthiouracil Side Effects

Seek emergency help if you experience:

  • Allergic reactions (e.g., hives, difficulty breathing, swelling of the face or throat)
  • Liver damage symptoms (e.g., nausea, vomiting, jaundice, dark urine)
  • Severe skin reactions (e.g., rash, blistering, peeling)

Common side effects:

  • Nausea, vomiting, stomach pain
  • Itching, nerve pain
  • Joint or muscle pain
  • Swollen glands
  • Headache, dizziness, drowsiness
  • Swelling in hands or feet
  • Hair loss
  • Decreased sense of taste

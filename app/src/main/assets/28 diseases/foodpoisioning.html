Food Poisoning

Symptoms

  • Upset stomach
  • Vomiting
  • Diarrhea
  • Diarrhea with bloody stools
  • Stomach pain and cramps
  • Fever
  • Headache

Some less often symptoms:

  • Blurred or double vision
  • Headache
  • Loss of movement in limbs
  • Problems with swallowing
  • Tingling or numbness of skin
  • Weakness
  • Changes in sound of the voice

Complications

  • Dehydration: Severe loss of water, salts, and minerals, often caused by vomiting and diarrhea.
  • Systemic disease: Contaminants can cause widespread disease, especially in older adults and those with weakened immune systems.
  • Causes: Blood clots in the kidneys, bacteria in the bloodstream, meningitis, and sepsis.
  • Pregnancy complications: Miscarriage or stillbirth, sepsis in the newborn, and meningitis in the newborn.
  • Rare complications: Arthritis, irritable bowel syndrome, Guillain-Barre syndrome, and breathing difficulties.

Prevention

  • Handwashing: Wash hands with soap and water for at least 20 seconds after using the toilet, before eating, and after handling food.
  • Wash Fruits and Vegetables: Rinse fruits and vegetables under running water before eating, peeling, or preparing.
  • Wash Kitchen Utensils: Wash cutting boards, knives, and other utensils with soapy water after contact with raw meats or unwashed fruits and vegetables.
  • Cooking Meat: Cook whole meats and fish to at least 145°F (63°C) and let rest for at least three minutes. Cook ground meat to at least 160°F (71°C) and whole and ground poultry to 165°F (74°C).
  • Refrigerating or Freezing Leftovers: Put leftovers in covered containers in the refrigerator right after your meal. Cook leftovers safely by thawing frozen food in three ways: microwave, refrigerator, or cold water on the counter.
  • Discarding Unsafe Foods: Discard any food that isn't sure if it has been prepared, served, or stored safely.
  • Treating Moldy Food: Throw out baked foods with mold, moldy soft fruits and vegetables, nuts or nut products, and trim away mold from firm foods with low moisture.
  • Cleaning Your Refrigerator: Clean the inside of the refrigerator every few months with a cleaning solution of baking soda and water.

Treatment

Fluid replacement: Maintains body fluid balance, essential after vomiting or diarrhea to prevent dehydration. Severe dehydration may require hospitalization.

Antibiotics: Prescribed for severe disease or high risk of complications.

Antiparasitics: Targets parasites and is usually prescribed for parasitic infections.

Probiotics: Treatments that replace healthy bacteria in the digestive system.

Home Remedies

  • Allow stomach to settle before eating.
  • Replace fluids with water, sports drinks, juice, or broths.
  • Recommend rehydration fluids for children or serious illness risk.
  • Gradually eat bland, low-fat, easy-to-digest foods.
  • Avoid dairy products, caffeine, alcohol, nicotine, and fatty or highly seasoned foods.
  • Rest for recovery from illness and dehydration.

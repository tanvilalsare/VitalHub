Pneumonia

Symptoms

Common Symptoms:
  • Chest pain when you breathe or cough
  • Confusion or changes in mental awareness (in adults age 65 and older)
  • Cough, which may produce phlegm
  • Fatigue
  • Fever, sweating, and shaking chills
  • Lower than normal body temperature (in adults older than age 65 and people with weak immune systems)
  • Nausea, vomiting, or diarrhea
  • Shortness of breath

Risk Factors

  • Highest risk in children aged 2 and younger.
  • Risk increases in individuals aged 65 or older.
  • Hospitalization increases pneumonia risk, especially in intensive care units.
  • Chronic diseases like asthma, COPD, or heart disease increase pneumonia risk.
  • Smoking damages natural defenses against pneumonia-causing bacteria and viruses.
  • Weakened or suppressed immune system, including HIV/AIDS, organ transplants, chemotherapy, or long-term steroids, increases risk.

Complications

  • Bacterial in the bloodstream (bacteremia): Infection can spread to other organs, potentially causing organ failure.
  • Difficulty breathing: Severe pneumonia or chronic lung diseases may cause difficulty breathing in sufficient oxygen.
  • Pleural effusion: Fluid accumulation around the lungs may require drainage or surgery.
  • Lung abscess: Pus forms in lung cavity, usually treated with antibiotics. Surgery or drainage may be necessary to remove pus.

Treatment

Medications:
  • Antibiotics
  • Cough medicine
  • Pain relievers

Hospitalization

You may need to be hospitalized if:
  • You are older than age 65
  • You are confused about time, people, or places
  • Your kidney function has declined
  • Your systolic blood pressure is below 90 mm Hg or your diastolic blood pressure is 60 mm Hg or below
  • Your breathing is rapid (30 breaths or more a minute)
  • You need breathing assistance
  • Your temperature is below normal
  • Your heart rate is below 50 or above 100
Children may be hospitalized if:
  • They are younger than age 2 months
  • They are lethargic or excessively sleepy
  • They have trouble breathing
  • They have low blood oxygen levels
  • They appear dehydrated

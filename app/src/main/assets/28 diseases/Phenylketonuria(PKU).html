Phenylketonuria (PKU)

Symptoms

Common Symptoms:
  • A musty odor in the breath, skin, or urine.
  • Neurological problems such as seizures.
  • Skin rashes, like eczema.
  • Lighter skin, hair, and eye color than family members.
  • Unusually small head size (microcephaly).
  • Hyperactivity and intellectual disability.
  • Delayed development and mental health disorders.

Risk Factors

  • Genetics: Inherited from both parents who carry the gene mutation.
  • Ethnicity: More common in individuals of European descent.

Complications

  • Intellectual disability: Severe untreated PKU can lead to intellectual and developmental issues.
  • Neurological problems: Including tremors, seizures, and behavioral issues.
  • Dermatological issues: Eczema and other skin conditions are common.

Treatment

  • Maintain a lifetime diet with low phenylalanine intake.
  • Special PKU formulas are used to ensure proper nutrition.
  • Medications may be prescribed for some individuals to help manage phenylalanine levels.

Lifestyle and Home Remedies

  • Strict adherence to a low-phenylalanine diet is crucial throughout life.
  • Consult a nutritionist for personalized meal planning.
  • Regular blood tests to monitor phenylalanine levels.
  • Support from family and friends is important for managing the condition.
  • Stay informed about the latest research and treatments for PKU.

Common Cold Information

Cause

Virus

Recovery Period

7-10 days (might be longer for smokers)

Symptoms

  • Runny or Stuffy nose
  • Sore or scratchy throat
  • Cough
  • Sneezing
  • Generally feeling unwell
  • Slight body aches or a mild headache
  • Low-grade fever

Complications

  • Middle Ear infection
  • Sinusitis
  • Asthma

Prevention

  • Wash your hands
  • Clean and Disinfect
  • Cover your cough
  • Don’t share

Treatment

Care tips

  • Rest
  • Drink plenty of liquids
  • Humidify the air
  • Use saline nasal rinse

Medications

Pain Relievers

  • For adults – Acetaminophen (Tylenol, others), Ibuprofen (Advil, Motrin IB, others)
  • For children – Children’s Acetaminophen or ibuprofen
  • Decongestant nasal sprays
  • Cough Syrups

Home Remedies

  • Drink plenty of fluids
  • Sip warm liquids
  • Rest
  • Adjust Room temperature and humidity
  • Use a saltwater gargle
  • Try throat soothers (Ice chips, lozenges, hard candy)
  • Use a suction bulb for nasal sprays

Precautions for Medicines

  • Read and follow label directions for cough syrups in adults
  • Don't take two medicines with the same active ingredient, such as an antihistamine, decongestant, or pain reliever
  • Nonprescription cough and cold medicines aren't typically recommended for children
  • Adults can use decongestant drops or sprays for up to five days
  • Children younger than 6 years old shouldn't use decongestant drops or sprays
  • Do not give children or teenagers aspirin
  • Use children-strength, nonprescription pain relievers
  • For children younger than 3 months old, don't use acetaminophen until your baby has been seen by a health care provider
  • Don't give ibuprofen to a child younger than 6 months old or to children who are vomiting frequently
  • Use these medicines for the shortest time possible and follow label directions to avoid side effects

Medication Information and Side Effects

Acetaminophen

Acetaminophen is used to treat mild to moderate pain, moderate to severe pain in conjunction with opiates, or to reduce fever. Common conditions treated include headache, muscle aches, arthritis, backache, toothaches, sore throat, colds, flu, and fevers.

Common Side Effects:

  • Stomach pain (Upper Right Side)
  • Loss of appetite
  • Tiredness, Itching
  • Dark urine, Clay-colored stools
  • Jaundice

Directions for use:

  • Adults and teenagers at least 12 years old: Do not take more than 1000 milligrams (mg) at one time or more than 4000 mg in 24 hours
  • Children younger than 12 years old: Do not take more than 5 doses of children's formula acetaminophen in 24 hours

Ibuprofen

Ibuprofen is a nonsteroidal anti-inflammatory drug (NSAID). It works by reducing hormones that cause inflammation and pain in the body.

  • Ibuprofen is used to reduce fever and treat pain or inflammation caused by many conditions such as headache, toothache, back pain, arthritis, menstrual cramps, or minor injury.

Side Effects:

  • Changes in your vision
  • Shortness of breath
  • Swelling or rapid weight gain
  • A skin rash, no matter how mild
  • Signs of stomach bleeding
  • Liver problems
  • Kidney problems
